About Us

Learn about our history, mission, and the visionary behind Mission Charitable Trust

A registered, wholly Zimbabwean-owned trust improving healthcare access through Mobile Health Units in remote areas, irrespective of caste, creed, or social standing. We focus on treatment of ailments, health education on communicable diseases including HIV and AIDS and prevention of STIs , targeting young people and empowering youths through Nursing Assistant training since 2019.

Our Founder's Story

Mission Charitable Trust (formerly Mudavanhu Charitable Trust) was founded in 2019 by Retired ZNA Sergeant Shepherd Mudavanhu. He witnessed first hand healthcare challenges facing rural, hard-to-reach communities internationally during his years of service in Zimbabwe and abroad. After retirement, Shepherd dedicated his life to addressing these disparities. What began as a small initiative to provide basic medical care has grown into a comprehensive healthcare organisation serving thousands annually.

Nursing Assistant Training Program

Building healthcare capacity by training the next generation of nursing professionals

Empowering Communities Through Education

Our accredited Nursing Assistant Training Program provides comprehensive healthcare education to individuals from underserved communities, creating employment opportunities while addressing critical staffing shortages in rural healthcare facilities.

The 7-month program combines classroom instruction with hands-on clinical experience, preparing graduates for certification and employment in various healthcare settings.

To date, we have trained over 250 nursing assistants, with 85% securing employment in healthcare facilities within six months of graduation.

  • 7-month comprehensive program
  • Classroom and clinical training
  • Certification upon completion
  • Job placement assistance
  • Focus on rural healthcare needs
